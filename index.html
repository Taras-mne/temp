WHY NOT CLOUD ·THE THREE QUIET THINGS

Every cloud quietly does three things. Foldy does none.

We don’t host your files.

Once a cloud has your bytes, they live on its disks — its TOS, its subpoenas, its training set, its next price hike. Foldy never touches them. The folder stays on your laptop, with you.

DIRECT P2P · NO MIDDLE

We don’t read your files.

Clouds need to read your files to “optimize”, “scan”, and improve their model. We’re not in the loop — the connection is end-to-end between two machines. No middle, nothing to read.

END-TO-END · ZERO KNOWLEDGE

We don’t hold your files hostage.

Cancel any time. Because your files never lived on our servers, there’s nothing for us to delete, lock, or hold for ransom. Your folder stays on your drive — subscription or not.

FAQ ·TEN QUICK ANSWERS

Quick answers.

01 Where do my files actually live?
On your machine. Foldy doesn’t have a server full of your folders — it opens a direct connection between you and the person you’re sharing with. If you close your laptop, the share goes offline. When you open it again, it comes back.
02 Is it encrypted?
Yes. Every byte between the two devices is end-to-end encrypted over WebRTC’s DTLS-SRTP, keyed by an Ed25519 identity that lives on the device. Keys never leave your machine. Foldy can’t read your files even if we wanted to — the cipher is ChaCha20-Poly1305 and the trust model is TOFU, like SSH.
03 What happens when my computer is offline?
The share is paused. The recipient sees “sender is offline” until you come back online. There’s no “cached copy in the cloud” — that’s the whole point. Your folder, your machine, your availability.

04 Do I need an account?
Yes. You create a Foldy account with an email and password — it keeps your shares organized and recognizes you across devices. Registration opens in your browser for a quick captcha check, then you confirm your email once. After that you sign in right inside the app.
05 Is there a file size limit?
No artificial cap. The practical limit is the slower of the two internet connections and how much RAM you want a single transfer to use. Big folders just take longer to read file-by-file on demand — the same way they’d download from any source.
06 Can I share with multiple people at once?
Yes — you can attach multiple recipient devices to the same share. Paid plans differ by how many devices you can attach.
07 How will Foldy be priced?
Foldy will have paid plans, priced by the number of recipient devices on your share. Exact tiers are still being finalized — beta sign-ups get locked-in early pricing.
08 How is this different from Dropbox / WeTransfer / iCloud?
Those copy your files to their servers first. We don’t. Dropbox is a synchronized clone, WeTransfer is a temporary upload, iCloud is rented storage. Foldy is none of those — it’s a live window onto a folder that already exists on your drive.
09 What about Nextcloud or Syncthing?
Nextcloud is our closest cousin in spirit — same anti-cloud instinct. But the lift is real: you run a server, pick a host, install, configure clients, route a domain, keep it patched. And the files still copy through your box. Syncthing is genuinely peer-to-peer, no middle, but it’s sync — full mirrored copies on every device, configured ahead of time, running in the background. Foldy skips both shapes. It’s a live mount: nothing copies, the folder appears on the other side only while the share is open, set up with one short code.
10 Is beta open?
Beta is open. You can sign up above and start using Foldy now — we’re still adding people in waves to keep peer-discovery healthy, but the gate has moved from “wait for invite” to “you’re in.”
